# Deep Multimodal Learning with Missing Modality: A Survey

**Authors:** Renjie Wu, Hu Wang, Hsiang-Ting Chen
**Venue:** ACM Computing Surveys (Submitted 2024), available on arXiv
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.07825
**Citations:** Recent survey, highly relevant for future work

## Abstract
This comprehensive survey (updated late 2024/early 2025) focuses on methodologies for handling "Missing Modalities". This is directly linked to the user's interest in "Robustness" against dominant modalities. If a model relies too much on a dominant modality (e.g., Vision), it fails when that modality is missing. The survey categorizes methods into:
1.  **Data Augmentation:** Synthesizing missing data.
2.  **Feature Disentanglement:** Separating modality-specific features.
3.  **Robust Fusion:** Designing fusion mechanisms that handle missing inputs gracefully.

## Key Contributions
- **Taxonomy:** Detailed classification of "Missing Modality" problems.
- **Link to Dominance:** Discusses how dominance leads to poor performance on missing data.
- **Future Directions:** outlines open problems in robust multimodal learning.
